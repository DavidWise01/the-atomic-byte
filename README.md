The physics is textbook — orbital types s/p/d/f/g hold 1·3·5·7·9 orbitals (the odd ladder, 2l+1), and spin doubles each into the capacities 2·6·10·14·18. The honest ANALOG: every capacity is a binary word — 6=2+4, 10=2+8, 14=2+4+8, 18=2+16 — all of the form 2+4l, so bit 1 (the spin pair) is a LOCKED seal and the upper 'shadow' bits carry which orbital it is. One byte holds the whole atom: the biggest shell, 2n²=32, needs only 6 of 8 bits. Born from David's riff mapping a 0–10 'towers / sentinels / seals' scheme onto atomic structure — the odd ladder = the orbitals (real, 1:1), the powers of two = the binary/computer half, the two joined at the ×2 = spin = the single bit. Two-layer honest: the 2+4l regularity is a genuine structural echo, but 'atoms are binary' is flagged as only the trivial fact that every integer is a sum of powers of two — a lens, not a law.
